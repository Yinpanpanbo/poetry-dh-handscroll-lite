DIGITAL CLOSE READING · A MAP OF POETIC GRAMMAR

How do eight lines move from “empty” to “remain”?

The pauses are not decorative scenes. They follow syntax, sensory order, and the four-couplet structure, creating an interpretation that can be inspected and questioned.

01 · ENVIRONMENTrain · evening · autumn

The poem clears away ordinary noise and establishes a cool, newly washed atmosphere.

→
02 · PERCEPTIONbetween / over · shines / runs

The eye moves from pines above to stone below; still light answers flowing water.

→
03 · HUMAN TRACESsound → return · stir → boat

Sound and movement arrive before their human causes. The mountain becomes lived space.

→
04 · CHOICEfade → remain

Description turns into judgement: why can an autumn mountain outweigh spring flowers?

TEXTS IN DIALOGUE · ALLUSION AND RECEPTION

Why bring these texts into the exhibition?

The first three couplets ask what is present in the mountain; the last asks why one might stay. These three objects clarify the closing allusion, Wang Wei’s recurring techniques, and the later history of reading his poetry through painting.

A · PRIMARY TEXT / B · RECEIVED READING

Zhao Yinshi: return, or remain?

“王孙兮归来，山中兮不可以久留。”

Why here: “The wanderer may remain” is often read as reversing an earlier call to return from the mountains. The echo turns landscape description into a decision.

TRANSLATION WORKSHOP · MEDIUM AS ARGUMENT

Why turn the poem into a mineral-colour handscroll?

The pigments and the handscroll format are not evidence about Wang Wei’s original setting. They make our own act of translation visible: verbal sequence becomes spatial sequence, and reading becomes paced viewing.
